About

Tech

LiFi is a wireless communication technology similar to WiFi in purpose, but highly contrasting in its method, and effects. Like WiFi, LiFi transmits data to the user, allowing them to access the internet. LiFi serves many purposes, such as providing connections to the internet. LiFi’s purposes differ from WiFi’s in speed and connectivity. LiFi is significantly faster, and causes less problems for both the user and the provider.

“[LiFi has a] band frequency of 200,000 GHz, versus the maximum 5 GHz of the wifi, [which] is 100 times faster and can transmit much more information per second.”

While WiFi uses the radio spectrum, which is limited to certain frequencies, LiFi transmits the data through light waves. This creates a much quicker, clearer and more accessible route of data, reducing latency. LiFi can be used anywhere where there is a light, as “any light fitting can easily be converted into an internet connection point.”

LiFi’s uses are widespread, such as:

Smart Devices The capabilities and speed of a wide variety of smart devices in the home, such as speakers, windows, doors and more will be enhanced by LiFi’s improved speed and reduced latency. Another incredible use of LiFi with smart devices is products such as robotic vacuum cleaners, which can use LiFi to both better map out environments, and also connect more quickly. Other uses include interactive venues such as museum exhibitions.
Vehicles and Transportation As the world transitions towards autonomous and semi-autonomous vehicles, the importance of interconnectivity in vehicles cannot be stressed enough. Firstly, an environment with vehicles autonomously driving requires complete awareness of surroundings. LiFi can be beneficial at transmitting this data between vehicles, through the headlights and indicators. The LiFi data can also assist with products such as Google and Apple Maps, or services which locate parking spots in a parking lot. The effects of LiFi in car headlights can compound with LiFi in Traffic Lights, allowing for more effective traffic management.
Military and Security Due to the nature of LiFi, it cannot pass through walls, or travel extremely long distances. This means that it is:
1. More secure (Can’t be accessed due to physical restraints)
2. Nearly zero electromagnetic signature, which means it can’t detected by enemies
3. Unlicensed Spectrum—The light waves cannot be licensed by countries like radio waves currently are.
4. Unjammable signal

LiFi is currently extremely expensive for the consumer, due to a lack of integration into the market. LiFi installation in a home is estimated to cost $2,000. On their own, LiFi-enabled LED bulbs are only slightly more expensive, however devices don’t fully support the technology, raising the prices significantly. LiFi is sold through companies which offer both the hardware and the software. If LiFi is properly integrated into the used technology (devices) then LiFi technology can become a cheap, well-integrated and advanced technology.

Hardware and Usage:

LiFi requires two main components—a photodiode and a light source. The photodiode is a “transceiver that receives light signals and transmits them back”. The light source (LEDs) transmits data by emitting light.

LiFi is commercially available, however extremely expensive, and sometimes limited in use. It requires a connector to use, which is not ideal.
