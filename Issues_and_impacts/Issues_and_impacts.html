The mainstream production of LiFi technology has the capabilities to have a significant impact on individuals, society as a whole, and the environment. These impacts are both positive and negative, which leaves both consumers and producers to decide whether the positives outweigh the negatives, or vice versa.

There are a wide variety of people and companies who LiFi will affect. These effects range from small, less noticeable changes to revolutionary, life-changing effects on people, organisations and the environment.

Effects on Consumer:

Pros Cons
Speed
Due to a higher band frequency than WiFi, LiFi can transmit 100× more data per second. This can be beneficial to household users which heavily rely on the internet.		 Technical Difficulties
Due to the developing nature of the technology, many users would face issues with the connection and maintenance.
Ubiquity
Since LiFi can be integrated into any LED light source, the ability to use light fidelity can be abundant, such as in street lights or vehicle headlights.		 Interferences
Since LiFi depends on a direction line of sight data transmission, if there is an obstruction to the connection, such as a wall, then the data communication halts.
Security
LiFi’s transmissions are much more secure and less susceptible to data leaks or hacking.		 Dependability
LiFi’s technology would depend on the success of the light source, so if the lights stop working, so does the internet.
Affordability
When fully integrated into society, LiFI can be cheaply added when buying LED light bulbs. 		Costly
Especially during the development, LiFi is extremely expensive to install and maintain. Currently, LiFi requires both technology to go within the LED bulbs and USB connectors for the device. In addition, LiFi would cause more energy costs to the consumer with lights being on much more often.

While LiFi predominantly affects the consumer/individual, it also has a significant impact on society, the economy and technology.

Society and Economy:

Pros Cons
Reliable
LiFi is reliable for society, as it will not face issues such as internet service providers (ISPs) not fulfilling their WiFi speed promises. This also helps the government as they can spend less resources on the upkeep for WiFi and 5G, which provide worse speeds.		 Feasibility
Society is currently deeply rooted into WiFi, and a complete overhaul would be required if society were to convert to LiFi. This seems extremely unfeasible to occur.
Bandwith
The radio spectrum, on which the WiFi system is built upon, is extremely limited and congested. The light spectrum, which LiFi utilises, has more than 10000× more space, meaning it will reduce congestion in the limited radio spectrum.		 Work
Many workers currently depend on the WiFi systems, and might lose their jobs if LiFi overtook WiFi. As well, LiFi would also require workers for uptake, which aren’t currently existing.

Business and Technology:

Pros
Non-interference
There are services of great importance (such as aeroplanes and hospitals) which use technology dependent on radio and electromagnetic waves. This means that any other use of radio waves nearby can cause electromagnetic interference (EMI). LiFi provides a solution, by allowing other people to still use the internet while the important services still function appropriately.
Military
LiFi provides the military with a more secure, undetectable and unjammable framework for internet activities.
Vehicles
LiFi allows for vehicles to communicate with one another, which can be essential both in semi-autonomous vehicles’ safety procedures and for fully autonomous driving capabilities.
Underwater Communications
WiFi cannot be used effectively under water, due to the transmission of radio waves. Light waves are able to be transmitted underwater, allowing for effective data communication underwater.
Interconnectivity
While WiFi currently is beginning to provide a framework for an interconnected society, LiFi significantly develops the possibilities of smart devices and interconnectivity.

LiFi's revultionary promises can seem unrealistically perfect, but there are also environmental factors to consider.

Effects on Environment:

Pros Cons
Energy Efficient
LiFi’s technology is less energy dependent than WiFi. LiFi requires LEDs which are significantly better for the environment, both in production and usage.		 Wasted Infrastructure
If society transitions from WiFi to LiFi, there will be a large amount of wasted infrastructure, which will contribute to landfill and global warming.
Greener Internet
LiFi, both in production and employment, creates a greener, more sustainable internet, than compared to WiFi.		 Light Pollution
Since LiFi is dependent on light waves, it may cause a significant increase in light pollution if adopted.
