Developers

There are multiple organisations researching and developing LiFi. LiFi was first introduced and researched by Professor Harald Haas, though Alexander Graham Bell’s invention of the Photophone can also be seen as research into light data transmission. Currently, companies such as lifi.co and purelifi.com are extensively researching LiFi as well as selling the product, while many universities across the world invest in LiFi research and development.

LiFi was first introduced by Professor Harald Haas in July 2011 at TED Global Talk. Professor Harald Hass and the University of Edinburgh coined “LiFi”, and developed the technology. Harald Hass was born in 1968 in Germany, and is a Professor of Mobile Communications at the University of Edinburgh. He was the cofounder of pureLifi, a leading developer of Light Fidelity.

Creation:

Traces of visual light communication (VLC) date back to the 1880s (the creation of the photophone), and are also seen in the Nakagawa Laboratories in the early 21st century. Since then, many companies have continued to develop LiFi’s technology.
